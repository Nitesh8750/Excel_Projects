
# US Sales Intelligence & Market Analysis

A robust Excel-based analytical framework designed to evaluate sales performance across the United States. This project identifies high-growth territories, optimizes product-mix strategies, and provides a granular view of seasonal revenue fluctuations to drive data-led business expansion.

---

## 🗺️ Project Roadmap

### 🔍 Phase 1: Data Integration & Integrity
* **Standardization:** Cleaned and formatted raw U.S. sales logs, ensuring consistency in regional naming conventions and currency formats.
* **Date Engineering:** Decomposed transaction dates into Year, Quarter, and Month to enable **Time Intelligence** reporting.

### 📈 Phase 2: Multi-Dimensional Analytics
* **Regional Benchmarking:** Built **Pivot Tables** to compare sales performance across the West, East, Central, and South regions.
* **Product Vitality:** Conducted a deep-dive analysis into product categories to distinguish "Volume Drivers" (high units sold) from "Value Drivers" (high revenue per unit).
* **KPI Development:** Engineered dynamic metrics for **Average Order Value (AOV)** and **Regional Market Share %**.

### 🎨 Phase 3: Strategic Dashboard Design
* **Sales Command Center:** Created a centralized dashboard featuring high-level KPIs and YoY growth indicators.
* **Interactive Filtering:** Integrated **Slicers and Timelines** to allow stakeholders to pivot between specific states, categories, or timeframes instantly.
* **Trend Visualization:** Developed combo charts and sparklines to visualize monthly performance against quarterly targets.

---

## 🚀 Strategic Insights & Business Value
* **Territorial Growth:** Identified a 15% revenue gap in the Central region, highlighting an opportunity for targeted marketing or sales force expansion.
* **Seasonality Trends:** Discovered a consistent Q3 peak across all regions, providing a data-backed justification for pre-season inventory stockpiling.
* **Product Mix Optimization:** Found that 20% of products generated 70% of the total revenue (Pareto Principle), suggesting a shift in focus toward high-margin SKUs.
